# Design System Document

This document outlines the core aesthetic and functional principles of our user interface, derived from our design token system.

## Brand Identity

### Color Palette
Our brand colors are carefully selected to evoke specific emotions and ensure brand recognition.

-   **Primary Color:** `#2D5016`
    -   This is our brand's most distinctive color, suitable for primary calls to action, important headings, and key interactive elements. It reflects our core identity.
-   **Secondary Color:** `#4A7C5F`
    -   A supportive color used for less prominent UI elements, secondary actions, and informational components. It complements our primary color without overshadowing it.
-   **Tertiary Color:** `#D4845A`
    -   An accent color used for highlights, badges, or decorative elements that require a distinct visual pop.
-   **Neutral Color:** `#5A5A55`
    -   A foundational color for backgrounds, surfaces, and non-chromatic elements, providing a stable and balanced canvas for the UI.

### Color Mode
Our interface is designed for optimal viewing in a **light** environment, ensuring legibility and comfort across various devices and conditions.

## Typography

### Headline Font
-   **Family:** Noto Serif
-   **Purpose:** Used for all major headings and titles, providing a strong and elegant presence that captures attention.

### Body Font
-   **Family:** Manrope
-   **Purpose:** Applied to all standard paragraph text, ensuring readability and a modern feel for longer content blocks.

### Label Font
-   **Family:** Manrope
-   **Purpose:** Dedicated to labels, captions, and smaller textual elements, maintaining consistency and clarity throughout the interface.

## Spatial Relationships

### Roundedness
Our UI elements feature **moderate (2)** rounded corners, striking a balance between modern aesthetics and approachability. This subtle curve provides a soft touch without losing structural integrity.

### Spacing
The layout employs a **normal (2)** level of spacing, creating a balanced and comfortable visual experience. This ensures elements have enough room to breathe without feeling sparse or overly dense.
